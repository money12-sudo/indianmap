Commercial Concentration and Residential Dispersal in South Asian Houston, 1973–2026

Indian Houston starts on Hillcroft in the 1980s and moves west to Sugar Land, Stafford, and Katy. Every site is plotted by year and by its distance from the corner of Hillcroft and Harwin. If the community relocated, the points should drift west over time and the core should empty out. Watch what actually happens.

A former India Culture Center president told Texas Highways that most Indian Houstonians live in Sugar Land, Pearland, and Clear Lake — and that everyone still comes back to Hillcroft, which he called a weekend go-town.

Ramesh Cherivirala, quoted in Texas Highways, 2022

The drift test

Horizontal: year established. Vertical: straight-line miles west of Hillcroft & Harwin. A migration would show the whole cloud climbing away from the baseline. What you see instead is a core that keeps adding sites at zero miles while a second cloud opens up ten to twenty miles out — two centers, not one moving one.

Status: starter dataset of records, not a finished archive. Dashed markers have approximate coordinates and must be field-verified before publication. Records marked lead are not yet facts.
